\section{Pairing test}\label{sec:pairing}

\paragraph{Metric.} Genome-wide $\alpha/\beta$ pairing is close to
random~\cite{Shcherbinin_2020}, but given an epitope the V/J usage may be constrained.
For every epitope with $\ge 30$ paired records we measure two signals as the
\emph{excess over a permutation null}, in bits, then average over epitopes
($\pm$95\% CI):
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{gene-usage bias}: $\overline{\mathrm{KL}}(P_e\,\|\,Q)$ over axes
  $V\alpha,J\alpha,V\beta,J\beta$ ($P_e$ = within-epitope gene distribution, $Q$ =
  cohort background) minus its mean under size-matched random draws from $Q$.
\item \textbf{inter-chain MI}: $I\!\left((V\alpha,J\alpha);(V\beta,J\beta)\right)$
  within epitope (Miller--Madow) minus its mean under $\alpha\!\leftrightarrow\!\beta$
  shuffles (which keep the marginals but destroy coupling).
\end{itemize}
Both are $0$ under no signal and grow (in bits) with genuine per-epitope structure.
Writing $X$ for either statistic and $\{X^{(k)}_{\text{null}}\}$ for its $K$
permutation replicates, the excess is $\Delta X_e = X_e-\overline{X^{}_{\text{null}}}$
and the standardized effect is
\[
z_e=\frac{X_e-\overline{X_{\text{null}}}}{\mathrm{sd}(X_{\text{null}})}.
\]
We report the excess $\Delta X$ (in bits) rather than $z$, because for small epitopes
the null variance can collapse ($\mathrm{sd}\to 0$) and inflate $z$; the excess is
stable and keeps the natural unit.

\paragraph{Result.} Figure~\ref{fig:pair} shows both. The verified cohorts carry
substantial gene-usage bias (VDJdb-HQ $\hqGene$ bits) and real inter-chain coupling
(TCRvdb-true $\trueMi$ bits). immrep25 positives are near the floor on both:
gene-usage bias $\immGene$ bits and, decisively, an inter-chain coupling of only
$\immMi$ bits---an order of magnitude below every quality cohort and barely above the
AIRR and OLGA controls ($\olgaMGene$ / $\olgaMMi$ bits). The residual gene-usage bias is
consistent with the public, HLA-restricted composition of the positives rather than
epitope-specific pairing.
